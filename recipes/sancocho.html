Sancocho

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (1 kg) bone-in chicken (legs, thighs, or a whole cut-up bird)
  • 1½ lbs (700 g) beef short ribs or beef shank (with bone)
  • 14 cups (about 3.5 L) water or light chicken stock
  • 3 ears of corn, cut into 2-3 pieces each
  • 1 large white onion, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 medium potatoes (Yukon Gold or sabanera), peeled and chunked
  • 2 green plantains, peeled and cut into 2-inch chunks
  • 1 medium yuca (cassava), peeled and cut into 3-inch pieces
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and chunked
  • 1 small bunch fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 bunch scallions, chopped
  • Cooked white rice
  • Lime wedges
  • Avocado slices
  • Aji or hot sauce of choice

Steps

  • Build the flavor bomb: Big stockpot, medium-high heat. Add water/stock, beef, chicken, corn, onion, garlic, bay leaves, cilantro stems, a generous pinch of salt, and pepper.
    Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Skim any foam. Cook about 45 min, until the beef starts to tenderize.

  • Add the hearty stuf: Toss in plantain chunks and yuca. Simmer 20 min..

  • Potatoes & carrot finale:Add potatoes and carrot, plus ground cumin if you like. Cook until all roots are tender—about 20 more min Remove cilantro stems, onion chunks, and bay leaves.

  • Finish the proteins:Pull out chicken and beef. Shred or slice into large rustic pieces and return to the pot. Adjust salt and pepper

  • Serve it:Ladle sancocho into deep bowls with a piece of corn and plenty of broth..

    • Sprinkle with chopped cilantro and scallions.
    Serve with a side of white rice, avocado, lime wedges, and a dab of aji or hot sauce.
